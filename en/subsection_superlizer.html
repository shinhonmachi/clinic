How Super Lizer Works
Near-Infrared Stellate Ganglion Irradiation

The stellate ganglion is a sympathetic nerve ganglion located at the base of the neck (near the cervicothoracic junction), governing the sympathetic nerves across a wide area including the head, face, upper limbs, and chest. Irradiating this area with near-infrared light eases sympathetic nerve tension, and can be expected to improve blood flow, balance hormones, and reset the autonomic nervous system as a whole.

Traditionally, treatment of the stellate ganglion has mainly involved an injection of local anesthetic (stellate ganglion block), but Super Lizer is used across a wide range of specialties — pain clinics, gynecology, internal medicine, and more — as a treatment that aims to achieve a similar effect non-invasively from the surface of the skin.

Comparison with Stellate Ganglion Block
For those who want to know how it differs from an injection

Super Lizer is a treatment that "aims for an effect similar to a stellate ganglion block without an injection." Which option is right for you depends on the severity of your symptoms and your constitution. Please consult us first.

Item Super Lizer Stellate Ganglion Block Injection
Treatment method Near-infrared light irradiated from the skin surface Local anesthetic injected near the ganglion
Invasiveness None (non-invasive) Yes (needle insertion)
Pain Almost none — just a sense of warmth Temporary pain or pressure at the time of injection
Treatment time About 3-5 minutes per side A few minutes (15-30 minutes including preparation and observation)
Who it's for Those who dislike injections, want to try it first, or want to attend regularly Those with severe symptoms or seeking a faster effect
🌿 Consultation Process

On your first visit, we will ask about your symptoms and concerns and confirm whether Super Lizer is appropriate. The treatment simply involves placing the device at the base of the neck (over the stellate ganglion area) and irradiating near-infrared light. There is no pain, and most patients feel a gentle warmth.

You can go home right after the treatment. Generally, continuing treatment once or twice a week makes the effects easier to notice. It can also be combined with other self-pay treatments (such as placenta injections or IV drips), so please feel free to ask.

Please feel free to consult us about chronic concerns such as autonomic imbalance, coldness, or menopausal symptoms.
