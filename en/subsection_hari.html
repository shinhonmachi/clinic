Self-pay treatment | Japan-born scalp acupuncture recognized worldwide
YNSA is short for Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture, developed by Dr. Toshikatsu Yamamoto, a physician in Miyazaki Prefecture. While still relatively unknown in Japan, it is already a highly regarded treatment practiced by thousands of doctors worldwide, and its outstanding effectiveness is widely recognized internationally.

High effectiveness has been reported across a wide range of conditions — from central nervous system disorders following stroke to low back pain, stiff shoulders, knee pain, numbness, dizziness, and tinnitus. Our clinic director has traveled to Miyazaki City, the birthplace of this treatment, to train directly under Dr. Yamamoto, and offers authentic YNSA.

A Japan-born Acupuncture Treatment Recognized Worldwide
YNSA — Yamamoto New Scalp Acupuncture

YNSA is a "Made in Japan" acupuncture method with a consistent theory and proven track record. It has its own reflex zones and diagnostic methods distinct from Chinese scalp acupuncture, and its highly reproducible effects regardless of the practitioner's individual skill are why it is supported by doctors around the world.

As the name "scalp acupuncture" suggests, needles are inserted into the scalp, using extremely fine 0.25mm diameter needles. Because the insertion is into the scalp, most people feel almost no pain. YNSA is also a treatment system distinct from conventional acupuncture, which uses meridians and acupoints (so-called "tsubo").

Theory and Features of YNSA
Theory & Features

Basic YNSA treatment uses nine basic points (reflex zones), labeled A through I, located on the head. These new acupoints discovered by Dr. Yamamoto and his predecessors correspond to conditions throughout the body, and a unique diagnostic method is used to identify the points that need treatment.

✓
Unique reflex zones: A reflex zone system distinct from Chinese scalp acupuncture, enabling treatment based on a consistent theory.
✓
Unique diagnostic method: Palpation of the neck and abdomen objectively identifies the points requiring treatment. In some cases, treatment effects can be confirmed on the spot.
✓
Nearly painless: Because extremely fine 0.25mm needles are used on the scalp, there is almost no pain. Treatment can be done using the scalp alone, without inserting needles into the trunk or limbs.
✓
Fast-acting: Many patients feel improvement during or immediately after treatment, making it a relatively quick-acting therapy.
