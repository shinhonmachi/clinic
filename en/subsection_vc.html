High-Dose Vitamin C
High-Dose Vitamin C IV Drip
Self-pay treatment | Anticancer support, immune boost, skin brightening, collagen production
Vitamin C, whose blood concentration cannot be raised significantly through ordinary diet or supplements, is supplied directly at high concentration via IV drip. When blood vitamin C concentration exceeds a certain threshold (about 0.4mM), it has been reported to exert a selective anti-tumor effect by generating hydrogen peroxide in cancer cells, and is used as a supportive cancer treatment at clinics around the world.

We propose dosing tailored to your goals, from low-to-moderate doses for beauty, immunity, and fatigue recovery, to high doses (25g-75g) for supportive cancer care.
⚠️ The treatment described on this page is self-pay (not covered by insurance). It cannot be administered to patients with G6PD deficiency (risk of hemolytic anemia). A G6PD test is always performed before high-dose administration. Patients with kidney failure or on dialysis should also consult us. Results vary from person to person.

Main Effects of High-Dose Vitamin C
Anticancer · Immune · Beauty · Collagen

Vitamin C is widely known as a water-soluble antioxidant vitamin, but when high concentrations are reached via IV drip, specific effects not obtainable through ordinary oral intake appear.

✓
Selective anti-tumor effect (at high doses): Generates hydrogen peroxide in the blood, selectively damaging cancer cells with low catalase activity. Toxicity to normal cells is low, and it combines well with chemotherapy.
✓
Immune boost: Increases the activity of NK cells, neutrophils, and lymphocytes, strengthening resistance to viruses and bacteria. Effective for recurrent infections and easy fatigue.
✓
Promotes collagen production: Acts as an essential coenzyme for collagen synthesis, promoting skin firmness, elasticity, and wound healing. A foundation for skin beauty and anti-aging.
✓
Antihistamine & anti-inflammatory effects: Eases allergic symptoms and inflammatory conditions. Also considered effective for hay fever and atopic dermatitis.
✓
Fatigue recovery & stress resilience: Helps synthesize adrenal cortex hormones (cortisol), supporting stress response and fatigue recovery.

📄 Research Papers & Evidence
Genes & Diseases (peer-reviewed international journal) | Review (analyzing over 150 studies) | published online 2025, in print 2026
High-dose vitamin C: A promising anti-tumor agent, insight from mechanisms, clinical research, and challenges

A comprehensive recent review (2025) by a surgical team at Capital Medical University (Beijing) analyzing over 150 prior studies. It establishes that pharmacological blood concentrations of vitamin C (20-30 mM) can only be achieved through intravenous administration, and organizes the four anti-tumor mechanisms that operate at this concentration, described below. It also notes that clinical data is accumulating showing the potential for reduced side effects (such as fatigue and gastrointestinal symptoms) and improved quality of life when combined with chemotherapy or radiotherapy, while concluding that larger trials are still needed regarding optimal dose, administration method, and patient selection.

① Selective Cytotoxicity (Pro-oxidant Effect)
Reacts with iron ions to generate hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂), selectively damaging cancer cells with low catalase activity while keeping toxicity to normal cells low.
② Epigenetic Regulation
Reactivates TET enzymes to restore expression of tumor suppressor genes. Suppresses tumor cell proliferation by reversing abnormal DNA methylation.
③ Suppression of Cancer-Related Signaling Pathways
Functions as a coenzyme for the enzyme that degrades HIF-1α (a transcription factor controlling tumor aggressiveness), blocking tumor growth and metastasis signaling.
④ Immune Activation
Strengthens the function of immune cells such as NK cells and T cells, enhancing the immune response within the tumor microenvironment.
Source: Zhao H, et al. Genes & Diseases 13(2):101742 (2026). DOI: 10.1016/j.gendis.2025.101742
CMAJ (Canadian Medical Association Journal) | Case report | 2006
Intravenously administered vitamin C as cancer therapy: three cases

Reports unexpected long-term survival or tumor shrinkage observed after high-dose vitamin C IV therapy in three cases of kidney cancer, bladder cancer, and B-cell lymphoma. A pioneering case report discussing its supportive role alongside standard treatment.

Frontiers in Oncology | Systematic review | 2020
Intravenous High-Dose Vitamin C in Cancer Therapy

The safety and tolerability of high-dose vitamin C IV drip have been confirmed in multiple Phase I/II trials, with potential for improved quality of life and reduced side effects when combined with chemotherapy. It also organizes the mechanism by which it acts selectively on cancer cells without toxicity to normal cells.

Dosage & Pricing

The dose is selected according to your goals. For cosmetic and immune-boosting purposes, 25g is typical; for supportive cancer care, 50-75g administered once or twice a week is typical. Before high-dose administration (50g or more), a test for G6PD (glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase) deficiency is required (first time only).

🌿 Consultation Process

On your first visit, we will ask about your goals, current treatment status, and medications during the interview. If you wish to receive 50g or more, a G6PD test (blood draw) will be done first.

For those visiting for supportive cancer care, bringing your current physician's treatment plan and test data allows us to make more specific recommendations. We also welcome consultations from those who find it difficult to discuss this with their primary physician.

Combination with other IV drips is also possible.
