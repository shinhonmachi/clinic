Myers' Cocktail
Myers' Cocktail IV Drip
Self-pay treatment | Fatigue recovery, immune support, autonomic balance, migraines
Myers' Cocktail is a comprehensive vitamin and mineral IV therapy developed and administered by Dr. John Myers in Baltimore, USA, in the 1970s. Vitamins and minerals are supplied directly via IV, aiming to improve a range of conditions including chronic fatigue, lowered immunity, autonomic imbalance, migraines, and fibromyalgia.

Unlike oral supplements, it can deliver high concentrations directly into the bloodstream without passing through the digestive tract, allowing for a quicker effect. At our clinic, we explain the current evidence on effectiveness and safety honestly, and carefully administer the treatment to those who wish to receive it.
⚠️ The treatment described on this page is self-pay (not covered by insurance). Please consult us in advance if you have kidney failure, serious heart disease, or hypokalemia. Results vary from person to person.

Our Approach to Safety Management
Safety Management & Medical Supervision

It is important that Myers' Cocktail be administered appropriately under a doctor's supervision. Our clinic has put the following measures in place to provide a safe and reassuring environment for treatment.

✓
Individually adjusted infusion speed: We administer the drip slowly, tailored to each patient's condition, to minimize the warmth or flushing sensation caused by magnesium.
✓
Thorough pre-treatment interview: On your first visit, we check kidney function, heart conditions, electrolyte balance, and current medications, and the doctor determines whether the treatment can be safely administered.
✓
Monitoring during administration: A nurse checks your condition periodically during the IV drip, and we are set up to respond immediately if any concerning symptoms arise.
✓
Cross-checking with current medications: We check in advance for combinations with medications prone to interactions, such as blood-pressure drugs, diuretics, and erythropoiesis-stimulating agents.
✓
For pregnant or breastfeeding patients: Because safety data is limited, we suggest alternative nutritional support methods for these patients.

Drug Interactions
Drug Interactions

If you are currently taking or using any medication, please be sure to inform us at your visit. Interactions have been reported in particular with the medications below.

Medication / Substance Possible Interaction
Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents
(epoetin, darbepoetin, etc.)		 Concurrent use with intravenous vitamin B6 may reduce the response to red blood cell production. Patients with chronic kidney disease or undergoing cancer treatment should be especially cautious.
Blood pressure medications
(calcium channel blockers, ARBs, etc.)		 Interaction with magnesium may cause blood pressure to drop further.
Ceftriaxone (injectable antibiotic) Combination with calcium can form insoluble precipitates, potentially damaging the kidneys, lungs, or gallbladder. We do not co-administer this during the IV drip.
Diuretics & alcohol (heavy consumption) May disrupt electrolyte balance (magnesium, potassium), which can affect the safety of the IV drip.

If you are taking any other medication not listed above, please let us know during the interview at your visit. The doctor will confirm safety before proceeding.

Method of Administration
Dosage

Administered slowly via IV drip over approximately 30-60 minutes. Adjusting the infusion speed minimizes the temporary vasodilation sensation (warmth or flushing) caused by magnesium. Many patients begin to feel relaxed and notice reduced fatigue even during the drip.

We also offer a Super Myers' drip with added glutathione (3A), depending on your symptoms and goals.

🌿 Consultation Process

On your first visit, we will ask about your symptoms, goals, current medications, and medical history, determine whether the treatment is appropriate, and then administer the drip. Please allow approximately 60-90 minutes total, including the interview and treatment.

For chronic fatigue or lowered immunity, continuing once weekly for 3-5 sessions makes the effects easier to establish. After that, we recommend maintenance sessions once or twice a month.

Please inform us in advance if you have kidney failure, are on dialysis, have serious heart disease or hypokalemia, or are pregnant or breastfeeding. If you are currently taking any medication, please bring something that shows the medication names (such as a medication notebook).

If you feel like your fatigue never goes away, or you want to fundamentally improve your overall condition, please reach out. Our doctor will provide careful counseling and recommend a drip tailored to you.
